Classification Rating the fine print

We at ToS;DR aim to create a transparent and peer-reviewed process to rate and analyse Terms of Service and Privacy Policies in order to both make Terms of Service more simple, aswell as rate, curate and keep track of them.

How we review

When a new service is added to the database, its Terms are reviewed by contributors, who divide the pertinent documents (ToS, Privacy Policy, Cookie Policies, etc.) into small quotes ("points"), each referring to a specific Case which will assign one of the following badges to the point:

+ Good 
- Bad 
× Blocker 
→ Neutral 

Each point will be up on our Database for subsequent review. If a point is approved by our site's Curators, the point will be approved.
Once a Service has enough points indexed to it, a Class is assigned automatically by averaging scores and its badges.

Classes

Class A The Service treats you fairly, respects your rights and will not abuse your data.

Class B  The Service is, for the most part, respectful towards the User, but its privacy practices could be improved.

Class C  The Service is still safe to use, but some issues might need your consideration.

Class D  The Service's practices are concerning and your data may be at risk.

Class E  The Service raises very serious concerns regarding its practices.

No Class Yet  The legal documentation hasn't been sufficiently reviewed.

Why do many services lack a Class?

You may have noticed that some services lack a class. This happens because, for a Service to be properly given a class, we need more data and more reviews before we can start assigning them one.

Moreover, you may also want to check how Classes are calculated, aswell as our Review Checklist.
